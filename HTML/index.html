Project Title: Estimating above ground biomass in Forest Plantations

Estimating above-ground biomass (AGB) in forest plantations is crucial for understanding various ecological and environmental factors. It helps quantify the amount of carbon stored in trees, which plays a key role in climate change mitigation efforts. By assessing AGB, we can gain valuable insights into forest health, growth, and productivity, and detect early signs of environmental stress or disease. This data is also instrumental in making sustainable forest management decisions, such as determining optimal harvesting practices and ensuring long-term forest vitality. Additionally, AGB estimates are used to assess biodiversity and habitat quality, as richer biomass often correlates with more diverse ecosystems. Beyond ecological monitoring, AGB is vital for the forestry industry, aiding in resource estimation and guiding timber production. Through the use of remote sensing and GIS technologies, AGB can be measured on a large scale, making it easier to monitor forest conditions across vast areas with minimal fieldwork. This data also has applications in renewable energy, as biomass can serve as a sustainable energy source. Ultimately, estimating AGB enhances our understanding of how forests respond to climate change and informs global environmental modeling efforts.
